Background

Growing up in a small town in Malaysia, Computer Science was a foreign and unfamiliar field to me. Studying at UCLA has exposed me to the wonders of Computer Science. Throughout my 2 years at UCLA, I've grown from being someone with no coding experience to someone who doesn't go a day without coding or building devices. My goal currently is to learn as much as possible about software and computer development.
Beyond coding and building devices, I have developed a passion for teaching, mainly due the influence of my parents who are both high school teachers. Incorporating both coding and teaching, I have organized various workshops in rural parts of Malaysia who have minimal access to coding resources. In addition, I was fortunate enough to meet fellow enthusiasts who share the same passion for teaching as I do. Together, we founded a non-profit organization, UniAns that organizes workshops around the country to educate high school students on higher education.

PROJECTS

Quadcopter

The Quadcopter project was a year long effort and can be broken down into three main phases, namely, the design phase, assembly phase and coding phase. The design process includes schematic design, component selection and a vigorous design review session. After 10 weeks, our customized PCB was fabricated and the next phase begun. The assembly process was the most frustrating and tiring of them all as I was new to SMD Soldering. Aside the constant inhalation of plumbum, my team could not afford to make any mistakes as the components were extremely fragile. Tying everything together, the coding phase involved taxing firmware coding and testing.

Object Recognition - Project VISION

Project Vision aims to improve the lives of the visually impaired by utilizing the power of computer vision and machine learning.
